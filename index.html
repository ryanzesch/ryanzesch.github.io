Hi, I'm Ryan Zesch! I am Computer Science PhD candidate at Texas A&M University. My research interests include physics simulation of deformable objects, collision detection, and garment simulation, often with neural approaches. I have completed internships at Amazon and NVIDIA, as well as international research in Japan through the NSF IRES program. I previously graduated summa cum laude from California Polytechnic State University, San Luis Obispo, with degrees in Mathematics and Computer Science.

In my spare time, I enjoy baking and cooking, going to concerts, and (go figure) playing games. I like to travel, and I take way too many photos of my cats.
